Section 2. Powers, Abilities, and Documented Limits
2.1 Core Mechanic: Inertial Suspension
The asset's primary power, designated Inertial Suspension (IS) under PAMC scientific cataloguing,
permits the targeted nullification of inertia in a single physical object within line-of-sight range. The effect
was first observed during the powers-emergence event of 8 October 2018 (see §3.4) and has been
continuously studied by the Cooperative Bureau of Metahuman Physics (CBMP) since March 2019.
The operative theory of mechanism — the Calvache–Marrón Equations — was developed jointly by the
asset and CBMP physicist Dr. Léon Marrón-Saavedra and published in restricted form on 11 May 2022.
The equations posit a transient local discontinuity in the conserved-momentum tensor, sustained by
metabolic substrate drawn from the asset's central nervous system. The discontinuity is bounded in
mass, duration, range, and recurrence, all of which have been experimentally calibrated.
When the effect is active, the suspended object:
has effective inertial mass equal to approximately 0.4% of its rest mass relative to the local Earth
frame
retains its gravitational mass (small objects still fall in earth gravity, but with no resistance to lateral
force)
does not interact mechanically with the asset herself (collision exemption)
resumes its prior linear and angular momentum exactly upon termination (see Tail Momentum,
§2.6)

This focus is mediated by visual attention; auditory or proprioceptive targeting is not possible. The asset

2.3 Mass Ceiling
The controlled mass ceiling under standard operational conditions is 240 kg. This figure was established
through CBMP testing across five quarterly cycles between 2019 and 2022 and has remained stable
through 2026.
Recorded peak (uncontrolled): 380 kg, observed during the 2018 trigger event when she suspended a
fragmenting block of slate during her fall. The trigger event remains the sole recorded instance above
250 kg and is not reproducible under controlled conditions.
Above the 240 kg ceiling, attempted activation produces one of three outcomes:
1. Activation failure with neural feedback (mild migraine onset) — 41% of cases
2. Partial suspension of a fraction of the target (e.g., one face of a larger object) — 36% of cases
3. Full activation followed by Echo Lock failure mode (see §2.13) — 23% of cases
2.4 Duration
Baseline maximum sustained duration of a single suspension: 0.6 seconds. This figure is hard-capped
under normal physiological conditions and is independent of target mass within the 240 kg ceiling.
Duration extension is achievable via pharmacological augmentation. Under therapeutic dose of Calexin-7
(240 mg, twice daily, see §4.9), tested duration extends to 1.1 seconds. The Cooperative does not
authorize Calexin-7 doses beyond therapeutic levels; off-label dosing of 360 mg in 2022 yielded 1.4 s
duration but produced severe vasoconstrictive side-effects and is prohibited under Cooperative Medical
Standing Order 11.

2.6 Tail Momentum
When the suspension ends, the target resumes its prior linear and angular momentum vector exactly —
direction, magnitude, and rotation. This property is termed Tail Momentum and underpins the Tail-
Strike combat technique (see §5.4).
Tail Momentum has been measured to within ±0.03% accuracy in CBMP impulse-recovery trials. The only
documented deviation occurred during the Antofagasta incident (May 2021), when an asymmetric
thermal gradient applied to a suspended target produced a 2.8% lateral drift; this anomaly has not been
replicated under controlled conditions.

2.8 Contact Exclusion
The asset cannot suspend an object that she is in direct physical contact with at the moment of trigger.
The contact exclusion applies to:
Held objects (e.g., she cannot suspend a baton she is currently gripping)
Worn equipment
Surfaces she is standing on or pressed against
Other personnel she is touching
She can, however, suspend an object that she releases within ~0.3 seconds prior to trigger. The
standard tactical drill known as "Drop-Tune" exploits this: she releases a throwing baton, lets it travel
briefly, then suspends it mid-flight for repositioning (see §5.5).

2.11 Tertiary Ability: Hold
Hold is a low-mass, extended-duration variant of Inertial Suspension available for objects up to 2 kg. It
permits sustained suspension for up to 4.0 seconds at a metabolic cost approximately equal to a single
full-duration standard suspension.
Hold is restricted to objects she has already successfully suspended once within the past 90 seconds;
Hold is a "re-tune" of an existing inertial signature, not a primary engagement. It cannot be applied to
living tissue and cannot be applied to self.
Operational use: keeping a thrown baton mid-air to choreograph a multi-baton kinetic field (the
Suspended Field technique, §5.6).

2.20 Documented Anomalies and Unresolved Mechanics
Four anomalies remain formally classified as Unresolved Mechanics by CBMP and are flagged for future
research cycles:
1. The Antofagasta Drift (May 2021): the singular 2.8% lateral deviation in Tail Momentum recorded
during the Drift Read awakening event. Hypothesis: asymmetric thermal gradient from a nearby
refinery flare. Replication attempts in CBMP labs (4 attempts, 2021–2024) all failed to reproduce
the effect.
2. The "Twin-Lock" hypothesis: during the 14 August 2023 Salta engagement, telemetry recorded a
0.21-second period in which two simultaneously suspended objects exhibited interlocked
termination — both released within 14 milliseconds of each other despite separate activation
timestamps. No mechanism has been proposed.
3. The 2024 Coquimbo dampening: during the 4th Tropopausa engagement (Coquimbo, May
2024), the asset's targeting failure rate dropped to 0.0% across 7 activations — well below the
0.4% baseline. Hypothesis: synergistic interaction with Tropopausa's pressure field. Unverified.
4. REM-cycle bleed: during REM sleep, the asset's Reset Bracers occasionally register low-
amplitude targeting-precursor signals despite no conscious activation. Frequency: ~3 per night. No
external effects have been observed; the phenomenon may be a subconscious artifact rather than
a true power expression.
These anomalies are reviewed each annual cycle and are not currently considered operational risks.

Section 5. Operational Tactics and Combat Doctrine
5.1 The Doctrine of Sequential Suspension
The asset's formal combat doctrine, the Doctrine of Sequential Suspension (DSS), was articulated by
the asset on 19 June 2022 in the aftermath of the Mendoza Pyrrhic engagement (§3.8). It is now PAMC-
recognized doctrine for Class A-2 (Targeted) operatives and forms part of the standard curriculum at the
Lima Operational Academy.
The doctrine rests on six core principles:
1. Suspension is a decisive resource, not a continuous capability. Treat each activation as
ammunition.
2. Sequence determines lethality. A single suspension rarely wins an engagement; ordered chains
of three define the doctrine.
3. Acquisition is the vulnerability. Protect the 1.2-second targeting window above all.
4. Tail momentum is the payoff. Plan terminal vectors before activating.
5. Cooldown discipline is non-negotiable. Operate as if the matrix is enforced by a third party.
6. Withdraw before exhaustion. Three activations and reset.

5.4 The Tail-Strike Technique
The Tail-Strike exploits Tail Momentum (§2.6) to deliver compounded impacts that cannot be achieved
by linear projectile motion alone:
1. The asset throws a baton at a target.
2. Mid-flight, she suspends the baton, halting it in space.
3. While suspended, she physically repositions the baton (e.g., rotating it, advancing it along its path
with a hand-push).
4. On release, the baton resumes its original momentum from its new position — striking with vector
consistent with original throw but originating closer.
The result: a strike delivered at full thrown velocity but from an arbitrary, target-adjacent location.
Adversary counter-prediction of trajectory becomes nearly impossible. Cumulative Tail-Strike
applications logged through 2025: 312 (§2.17).
5.5 The Drop-Tune Manoeuvre
Drop-Tune is the standard activation pattern around the contact-exclusion rule (§2.8):
1. Asset releases an object she is holding.
2. After ~0.3 seconds, the contact-exclusion field clears.
3. Asset suspends the released object before it strikes anything.
The technique permits a meaningful one-second window between release and engagement target
acquisition. The exact 0.3 s minimum has been internalized as muscle memory; the asset's Cell Halcón
training videos use a metronome cue to instill the rhythm in new operatives.
